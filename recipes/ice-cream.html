Ice Cream

Ice cream is a beloved frozen dessert that combines cream, sugar, and flavorings to create a rich and creamy indulgence. With its origins dating back centuries, this delightful treat has evolved into countless varieties, making it a staple in households and ice cream parlors around the world.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Whisk until the sugar is fully dissolved.
  2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a lid and refrigerate the mixture for at least 1-2 hours. For the best results, chill it overnight.

  3. If you have an ice cream maker, pour the chilled mixture into the ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturerâ€™s instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

    If you don't have an ice cream maker, pour the mixture into a shallow dish and place it in the freezer.

  4. After 30 minutes, take it out and stir vigorously with a fork or whisk to break up any ice crystals. Return to the freezer. Repeat this process every 30 minutes for about 2-3 hours, or until the ice cream is firm and creamy.
  5. Once the ice cream is firm, it's ready to serve! You can scoop it into bowls or cones and enjoy it plain or with your favorite toppings!
